Article 1: Ownership and Transfer Restrictions

1.1 Family-Only Ownership

Mlandeli Notemba Investments (Pty) Ltd shall remain 100% owned by direct descendants of Mlandeli Nelson Mdeni and Nozukile Cynthia Mdeni. No shares may be transferred to non-family members under any circumstances.

1.2 DNA Verification Requirement

All shareholders and beneficiaries must provide DNA verification of their lineage as descendants of the founding family. This requirement is applied equally and fairly to all family members, following the precedent established with Lukhanyo Sazi Mkosana.

1.3 Pre-emptive Rights

Before any shares can be transferred, existing shareholders have the right of first refusal to purchase those shares at fair market value as determined by independent valuation.

Article 2: Earn-In Structure and Performance Management

2.1 Merit-Based Advancement

Sisters (Visa, Tina, and Kwakho) begin with minimum shareholdings and earn their full intended allocation through:

  • Active participation in family business activities
  • Demonstrated contribution to ecosystem development
  • Completion of Sazi Life Academy professional development programs
  • Adherence to Ubuntu principles and family values

2.2 Performance Review Process

Annual performance reviews will be conducted by the Family Council, measuring:

  • Business contribution and value creation
  • Family unity and relationship building
  • Community involvement and Ubuntu demonstration
  • Personal development and learning achievements

Article 3: Next Generation Rights and Responsibilities

3.1 The Grandchildren's First Share (1% Rule)

Every direct descendant receives 1 share (0.01%) upon their 18th birthday as their birthright, held in trust until maturity. Current beneficiaries include:

  • Lukhanyo Sazi Mkosana - Born: March 28, 2018 (Son of Salatiso L. Mdeni)
  • Solonwabo Milile Mdeni - Born: March 18, 2010 (Nephew)
  • Milande Paton Mdeni - Born: May 5, 2017 (Nephew)
  • Future grandchildren and great-grandchildren

3.2 Vesting Schedule for First Generation

Special vesting schedule for Sazi, Solonwabo, Milande, and future children of current shareholders:

  • Age 25: Up to 25% of parental entitlement (performance-based)
  • Age 30: Additional 25% (cumulative 50%)
  • Age 35: Additional 25% (cumulative 75%)
  • Age 43: Final 25% (full inheritance achieved)

4.2 Family Council

The Family Council comprises all adult family members and meets quarterly to:

  • Review family member performance and contribution
  • Make decisions on share allocation and vesting
  • Resolve family disputes through Ubuntu principles
  • Plan for next generation development and education

Article 5: Business Operations and Risk Management

5.1 Primary Purpose

Mlandeli Notemba Investments serves as the family holding company for risk management purposes, owning:

  • 100% of Salatiso Ecosystem intellectual property
  • 100% of subsidiary operating companies (starting with SafetyHelp)
  • Family real estate and investment assets
  • Strategic partnerships and joint ventures

5.2 Subsidiary Company Strategy

As each ecosystem brand grows, it will be separately incorporated as a wholly-owned subsidiary:

  • SafetyHelp (Pty) Ltd - First target subsidiary (CEO: Visa Sande Mdeni)
  • BizHelp (Pty) Ltd - Business consulting and development
  • DocuHelp (Pty) Ltd - Document management and compliance
  • Additional subsidiaries as ecosystem expands

Article 6: Exit and Re-entry Provisions

6.1 Voluntary Exit

Family members choosing to disengage from family business:

  • May sell shares back to company at fair market value
  • Shareholding reduced to minimum legal amount for 10-year cooling-off period
  • After 10 years, remaining shares bought out if no return intention

6.2 Re-entry Process

Descendants of exited family members may return through:

  • Starting as employee to prove commitment and value
  • 10-year evaluation period after formal return intention
  • Eligibility for minimum shareholding after successful evaluation
  • Opportunity to earn greater share through family performance process
