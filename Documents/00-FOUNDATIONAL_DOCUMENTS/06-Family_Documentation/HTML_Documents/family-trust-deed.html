7. Powers of Trustees

7.1 Asset Management Powers

The Trustees shall have the power to:

  • Buy, sell, lease, or otherwise deal with immovable property
  • Invest trust funds in authorized investments and business ventures
  • Establish and manage subsidiary companies and business entities
  • Enter into partnerships and joint ventures for trust benefit
  • Employ professional advisors and service providers
  • Incur debt and provide security for trust obligations

7.2 Distribution Powers

The Trustees have absolute discretion to:

  • Distribute income to beneficiaries based on need and merit
  • Accumulate income within the trust for future distribution
  • Make capital distributions for beneficiary education, housing, or business needs
  • Advance funds to beneficiaries for approved purposes
  • Create sub-trusts for specific beneficiaries or purposes

7.3 Family Governance Powers

The Trustees shall:

  • Implement and oversee the Family Charter principles
  • Conduct annual family performance reviews
  • Facilitate family meetings and conflict resolution
  • Oversee the education and development of next-generation beneficiaries
  • Maintain family values and Ubuntu principles in all decisions

8. Trustee Duties and Responsibilities

8.1 Fiduciary Duties

  • Act in the best interests of all beneficiaries at all times
  • Exercise care, diligence, and skill in trust management
  • Avoid conflicts of interest and self-dealing
  • Keep accurate records and provide regular reporting
  • Maintain confidentiality of trust affairs

8.2 Family Responsibilities

  • Promote family unity and Ubuntu principles
  • Ensure fair treatment of all beneficiaries
  • Support beneficiary education and development
  • Plan for long-term family prosperity
  • Preserve family history and cultural heritage

9. Distribution Principles

9.1 Ubuntu-Based Distribution

All distributions shall reflect Ubuntu principles: "I am because we are." Individual needs are met within the context of family prosperity and community benefit.

9.2 Distribution Criteria

  • Need: Financial circumstances and life requirements
  • Merit: Contribution to family and community
  • Potential: Investment in education and development
  • Unity: Actions that strengthen family bonds
  • Values: Adherence to Ubuntu and family principles

10. Trust Governance and Amendment

10.1 Family Council Integration

The Trust shall operate in coordination with the Family Council established under the Family Charter, ensuring alignment between trust governance and family values.

10.2 Amendment Process

This Trust Deed may be amended by:

  • Unanimous consent of all Trustees
  • Approval of the Family Council where amendments affect beneficiary rights
  • Compliance with South African trust law requirements
  • Registration of amendments with the Master of the High Court

10.3 Successor Trustees

In the event of trustee retirement, death, or incapacity:

  • Replacement trustees shall be appointed by remaining trustees
  • Family trustees should be selected from beneficiaries showing leadership
  • Independent trustees must be qualified professionals
  • All appointments subject to Master of the High Court approval

11. Trust Termination and Vesting

11.1 Vesting Date

This Trust shall vest on the earlier of:

  • 100 years from the date of registration
  • The date determined by unanimous trustee resolution
  • The date when only one beneficiary remains

11.2 Final Distribution

Upon vesting, trust assets shall be distributed to beneficiaries based on:

  • Their shareholding in Mlandeli Notemba Investments (Pty) Ltd
  • Their historical contribution to family prosperity
  • Their role in maintaining family unity and values
  • The principle of Ubuntu and collective benefit
