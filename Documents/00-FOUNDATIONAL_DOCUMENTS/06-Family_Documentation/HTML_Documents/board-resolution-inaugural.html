Quorum: Present (2 of 2 directors)

RESOLUTIONS PASSED

RESOLUTION 1: CONFIRMATION OF DIRECTORS APPOINTMENT

IT WAS RESOLVED THAT:

  1. The appointment of Visa Sande Mdeni as Chief Executive Officer and Director of the Company is hereby confirmed, effective from the date of incorporation.
  2. The appointment of Salatiso Lonwabo Mdeni as Chairman and Director of the Company is hereby confirmed, effective from the date of incorporation.
  3. Both directors have confirmed their consent to act as directors and have declared that they are not disqualified from acting as directors in terms of the Companies Act.

Voting: Unanimous approval

RESOLUTION 2: ADOPTION OF COMPANY POLICIES

IT WAS RESOLVED THAT:

  1. The Company adopts Ubuntu principles as the foundation for all business operations and family governance.
  2. The Family Value quantification system shall be implemented for performance evaluation and advancement within the organization.
  3. All business partnerships shall be structured as family business relationships rather than employer-employee arrangements.
  4. The Company commits to evidence-based merit advancement over credential-based advancement.
  5. Community benefit and development shall be considered in all major business decisions.

Voting: Unanimous approval

RESOLUTION 3: BANKING AND FINANCIAL ARRANGEMENTS

IT WAS RESOLVED THAT:

  1. The Company is hereby authorized to open banking accounts with reputable South African banking institutions.
  2. Bank signatories shall be the Chief Executive Officer (Visa Sande Mdeni) and Chairman (Salatiso Lonwabo Mdeni).
  3. Any transaction above R50,000 requires dual authorization from both directors.
  4. Monthly financial reports shall be prepared and reviewed by the board.
  5. Annual audited financial statements shall be prepared as required by law.

Voting: Unanimous approval

RESOLUTION 4: SUBSIDIARY STRATEGY AUTHORIZATION

IT WAS RESOLVED THAT:

  1. The Company is authorized to establish wholly-owned subsidiaries for each business brand within the Salatiso Ecosystem.
  2. SafetyHelp (Proprietary) Limited shall be the first subsidiary to be established, with Visa Sande Mdeni appointed as CEO.
  3. Each subsidiary shall operate with its own board of directors while maintaining alignment with Ubuntu principles and family values.
  4. The Company shall provide management services to subsidiaries and receive management fees and dividend income.
  5. Intellectual property shall be licensed to subsidiaries through formal licensing agreements.

Voting: Unanimous approval

RESOLUTION 5: FAMILY GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

IT WAS RESOLVED THAT:

  1. A Family Council shall be established consisting of all adult family members to provide governance oversight.
  2. Quarterly family meetings shall be held to review company performance and family member development.
  3. Family member employment shall be based on merit, competence, and contribution to family values.
  4. Professional development through Sazi Life Academy shall be available to all family members.
  5. Succession planning shall consider both competence and commitment to Ubuntu principles.

Voting: Unanimous approval

RESOLUTION 6: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY MANAGEMENT

IT WAS RESOLVED THAT:

  1. The Company shall own all intellectual property rights related to the Salatiso Ecosystem.
  2. Patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets shall be registered in the Company's name.
  3. Licensing agreements shall be established for the use of IP by subsidiaries and external partners.
  4. IP protection and enforcement strategies shall be implemented to safeguard family assets.
  5. Regular IP audits shall be conducted to identify and protect new innovations.

Voting: Unanimous approval

Ubuntu Commitment

"In adopting these resolutions, we honor the legacy of Mlandeli Nelson Mdeni and Nozukile Cynthia Mdeni (Notemba), committing ourselves to Ubuntu principles: 'I am because we are.' Our success as individuals strengthens our collective prosperity, and our business shall serve not only our family but our broader community."
