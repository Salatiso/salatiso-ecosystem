üåçüåê The Internet vs Our Family Intranet

üåç What is the Internet?

The internet is like a huge public shopping mall with millions of stores, restaurants, and people. Anyone can go there, and you can find almost anything - good information, fun games, shopping, and learning. But just like a real mall, there are also some unsafe areas and people who might try to trick you.

üè† What is Our Family Intranet?

Our family intranet is like our own private home. It's a special network just for our family and trusted partners. Only people we invite can come in. It's where we keep our important family documents, business information, and tools that help us work together safely. Think of it as our digital family living room.

üåç The Internet

  • Public - anyone can access
  • Millions of websites and people
  • Free to use (mostly)
  • Can be unsafe sometimes
  • Like going to a big city

üè† Our Intranet

  • Private - only family invited
  • Just our documents and tools
  • Secure and safe
  • Always trustworthy
  • Like being at home

üì∂üîµ WiFi and Bluetooth Networks

üì∂ What is WiFi?

WiFi is like invisible radio waves that carry information through the air. It's how your phone, tablet, or computer connects to the internet without wires. In our ecosystem, we use special family WiFi networks that are extra secure and fast, so we can share information quickly and safely between our devices.

üîµ What is Bluetooth?

Bluetooth is like a short-range wireless connection between devices that are close to each other. It's perfect for connecting your phone to wireless earbuds, or sharing files with someone sitting next to you. In our family, we use Bluetooth for safe, direct connections between devices when we need to share information without going through the internet.

üõ°Ô∏è Internet Safety - Age Appropriate

üéÆ For Children (Ages 4-12): Simple Rules

  • Only talk to people your parents know about online
  • Never share your full name, address, or school name
  • Tell a grown-up if something feels wrong or scary
  • Only use apps and websites your parents approve
  • Remember: Real friends want to meet in person, not just online

üöÄ For Teenagers (Ages 13-17): Smart Choices

  • Think before you share photos or personal information
  • Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
  • Be careful about what you click - some links can be tricks
  • Report cyberbullying immediately to trusted adults
  • Remember your digital footprint lasts forever

üíº For Adults (Ages 18+): Professional Safety

  • Verify website security (look for https:// and lock icon)
  • Use different passwords for different accounts
  • Be cautious with email attachments and links
  • Regularly update your devices and apps
  • Use antivirus software and keep it current

üëµ For Elders: Simple Wisdom

  • If something seems too good to be true, it probably is
  • Never give money or personal information to unsolicited requests
  • Call family members directly to verify suspicious messages
  • Only use websites and apps recommended by family
  • Trust your instincts - if it feels wrong, it probably is

üîí Intranet Safety Tips for Productivity

üè† Keep Our Family Network Secure

  • Only connect to our family WiFi network at home
  • Use strong passwords for all family accounts
  • Don't share intranet access with outsiders
  • Log out when you're done using shared devices
  • Report any suspicious activity to family IT

üíº Work Safely on the Intranet

  • Only open documents from trusted family sources
  • Don't download unknown files or programs
  • Use your LifeCV to access appropriate content
  • Keep your trust score high by following rules
  • Back up important work regularly

üì± Device Safety Habits

  • Lock your devices when not in use
  • Keep apps and systems updated
  • Use antivirus protection on all devices
  • Don't leave devices unattended in public
  • Report lost or stolen devices immediately

ü§ù Family Communication Rules

  • Use Sonny to verify identities before sharing sensitive information
  • Discuss important decisions in person or video calls
  • Keep family business discussions on secure channels
  • Respect privacy - don't read others' messages without permission
  • Be kind and supportive in all digital communications
