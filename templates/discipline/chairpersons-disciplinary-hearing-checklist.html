Chairperson's Disciplinary Hearing Checklist

Employee: [Employee Name]

Date: [Date]

Before the Hearing

Confirm you are impartial and have no prior involvement in the case.

Ensure the employee received proper notice (at least 48 hours).

Review the charges; ensure they are clear and specific.

Review the investigation file and witness statements.

Opening the Hearing

Introduce all parties present (Initiator, Employee, Representative, HR Rep).

Explain the purpose of the hearing (to determine facts, not to punish).

Explain your role as an impartial chairperson.

Confirm the employee understands their rights (to representation, to state their case, to call/cross-examine witnesses).

Read out the charges and ask the employee if they understand them.

During the Hearing

Allow the Initiator (employer's representative) to present the company's case and call witnesses.

Allow the Employee and their representative to cross-examine the company's witnesses.

Allow the Employee to present their case and call witnesses.

Allow the Initiator to cross-examine the employee's witnesses.

Ask clarifying questions to both sides to ensure all facts are clear.

Allow both parties to present a closing statement.

After the Hearing

Adjourn the hearing to consider all evidence presented.

Make a finding of guilt or innocence based on the balance of probabilities.

If guilty, reconvene the hearing to hear arguments in mitigation and aggravation.

Decide on a fair and appropriate sanction, considering the principle of progressive discipline.

Communicate the outcome to the employee in writing, informing them of their right to appeal.
