Salary Negotiation Guide

Phase 1: Preparation is Key

1
Research Your Market Value: Use sites like Payscale, Glassdoor, and LinkedIn Salary to find the typical salary range for your role, experience level, and location. Note down the 25th, 50th, and 75th percentiles.
2
Quantify Your Accomplishments: Don't just list your duties; list your achievements. Use numbers. "Increased sales by 15%" is stronger than "Responsible for sales." Prepare a list of your key contributions to the company.
3
Define Your Target and Walk-Away Numbers: Know the ideal salary you're aiming for (your target) and the absolute minimum you're willing to accept (your walk-away point). Consider the full compensation package, including benefits, bonuses, and leave.

Phase 2: The Conversation

4
Schedule a Formal Meeting: Request a meeting with your manager specifically to discuss your compensation and career growth. Don't ambush them in a hallway.
5
State Your Case Calmly and Confidently: Present your research and accomplishments. Frame it as a discussion about your value to the company. Use phrases like, "Based on my research and my contributions, such as [X and Y], I believe a salary in the range of [Z] would be more aligned with my role and the market."
6
Listen and Be Flexible: After you've made your case, stop and listen. The initial response may not be a simple "yes." Be prepared to discuss other forms of compensation, such as a performance bonus, additional leave, or a professional development budget.
7
Get it in Writing: Once you've reached an agreement, ensure you receive a formal, updated offer letter or contract that reflects the new terms.
