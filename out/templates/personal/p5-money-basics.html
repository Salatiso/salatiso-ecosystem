Money is the fuel that keeps your business running. Understanding these basic concepts will help you make smart decisions and build a successful business that lasts.

üí∏ Money Flow: Income vs Expenses

üìä The Profit Formula

This is the most important equation in business. Memorize it!

Profit = Income - Expenses

Simple Example: You sell lemonade for R10 each. It costs R3 to make each glass.
Income: R10 | Expenses: R3 | Profit: R7 per glass
Sell 10 glasses = R70 income - R30 expenses = R37 profit!

üìù Types of Income & Expenses

üíµ Income Examples

  • Product Sales: Selling physical items you make or buy
  • Service Fees: Money for work you do (tutoring, repairs, etc.)
  • Subscriptions: Monthly payments for ongoing services
  • Commissions: Percentage of sales from partnerships

üõí Expense Examples

  • Materials: Raw materials, packaging, supplies
  • Tools & Equipment: Things you need to do your work
  • Marketing: Advertising, business cards, website
  • Transport: Getting to customers or delivering products

üí∞ Save First, Spend Later
