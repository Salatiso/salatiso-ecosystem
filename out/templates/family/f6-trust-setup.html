üèóÔ∏è How a Family Trust Works

The key roles and relationships in a trust structure

Founder/Donor

Creates the trust and transfers assets. May retain some control through trustee position.

Trustee

Manages trust assets and makes decisions according to trust deed. Can be individual or corporate.

Beneficiaries

Receive benefits from the trust. Can include current and future family members.

Trust Protector

Optional role to oversee trustees and ensure trust purposes are fulfilled.

Appointor

Has power to appoint/remove trustees. Provides additional control and flexibility.

Guardian

Cares for minor beneficiaries and ensures their interests are protected.

üìã Estate Planning Integration

How trusts fit into your overall estate planning strategy

1. Asset Inventory

List all assets: business interests, property, investments, personal possessions

2. Will Preparation

Create will appointing trustees and outlining final wishes

3. Trust Establishment

Set up trusts for asset protection and tax-efficient transfers

4. Beneficiary Designations

Update insurance policies and retirement accounts

5. Power of Attorney

Appoint agents for financial and healthcare decisions if incapacitated

6. Regular Reviews

Update plan annually and after major life changes

Essential Estate Planning Documents

  • Last Will and Testament: Distributes assets not in trusts
  • Trust Deeds: Govern trust operations and distributions
  • Power of Attorney: Financial and healthcare decisions
  • Living Will: Medical treatment preferences
  • Letter of Wishes: Personal guidance to trustees

üí∞ Tax Benefits of Family Trusts

Understanding the tax advantages available through proper trust structuring

Estate Duty Reduction

Assets in trust avoid estate duty on death. Only lifetime transfers may be subject to donations tax.

Capital Gains Tax Planning

Trust can hold assets long-term, potentially reducing CGT on disposal through annual exclusions.

Income Tax Efficiency

Trust income distributed to beneficiaries in lower tax brackets reduces overall tax burden.

Donations Tax Optimization

Annual R100,000 donations tax exemption per donor allows tax-free wealth transfer.

Business Asset Protection

Separate business assets from personal estate, potentially reducing overall estate tax.

Foreign Asset Planning

Offshore trusts can provide additional tax planning opportunities (consult tax advisor).

Important: Tax laws change frequently. Always consult a qualified tax advisor for current South African tax implications.

  • Estate duty reduction

  • Income splitting opportunities

Privacy Protection

Keep family wealth matters confidential

  • Trust details not public record
  • Avoid probate publicity

üìù Setting Up Your Family Trust

Step-by-step guide to establishing a trust for your family

1. Define Objectives

Clearly articulate what the trust should achieve: asset protection, tax planning, succession

2. Choose Trust Type

Select appropriate trust structure based on your goals and South African requirements

3. Select Trustee

Choose professional trustee or family member with proper qualifications

4. Draft Trust Deed

Work with attorney to create legally sound trust document

5. Transfer Assets

Move assets into trust ownership according to legal requirements

6. Register Trust

Register with Master of the High Court and obtain tax reference number

7. Establish Administration

Set up banking, record-keeping, and ongoing management systems

8. Regular Reviews

Annual review of trust performance and compliance

Cost Estimate: R25,000-R75,000 for setup (attorney fees, registration, professional trustee). Annual administration: R5,000-R15,000 depending on complexity.
