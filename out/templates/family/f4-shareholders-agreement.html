Dividend & Profit Distribution

  • Dividend payment policies
  • Equal vs performance-based distribution
  • Reinvestment requirements
  • Tax-efficient distribution methods

Management & Employment

  • Employment terms and conditions
  • Performance evaluation processes
  • Termination procedures
  • Compensation policies

Valuation Methods

How to determine share value for buying/selling

  • Independent valuation requirements
  • Regular valuation frequency
  • Dispute resolution for valuations
  • Pre-agreed valuation formulas

Dispute Resolution

How to handle conflicts between shareholders

  • Mediation and arbitration procedures
  • Escalation processes
  • Independent expert involvement
  • Buyout options for irreconcilable disputes

Understanding what ownership means in your family business

Voting Rights

How shareholders participate in decisions

  • One share, one vote (usually)
  • Major decisions require higher thresholds
  • Proxy voting for absent shareholders
  • Special voting rules for family matters

Information Rights

Access to business information and records

  • Regular financial reports
  • Annual general meetings
  • Access to company records
  • Management reporting requirements

Dividend Rights

Entitlement to share in company profits

  • Pro-rata distribution based on shares
  • Board discretion on dividend payments
  • Reinvestment options
  • Tax-efficient distribution methods

Pre-emptive Rights

Right to maintain ownership percentage

  • First right to buy new shares
  • Maintain proportional ownership
  • Protect against dilution
  • Funding requirements for exercise

Responsibilities

What shareholders must do to maintain rights

  • Capital contribution commitments
  • Confidentiality obligations
  • Non-compete agreements
  • Active participation requirements

Exit Rights

How shareholders can sell or transfer shares

  • Right of first refusal to other shareholders
  • Fair valuation requirements
  • Drag-along and tag-along rights
  • Compulsory buyout provisions

‚öñÔ∏è Resolving Shareholder Disputes

Fair processes to handle disagreements without destroying family relationships

1. Direct Discussion

Shareholders attempt to resolve issues through open conversation

2. Family Council Mediation

Neutral family members facilitate discussion and find common ground

3. Independent Mediation

Professional mediator helps parties reach mutually acceptable solution

4. Expert Determination

Independent expert (accountant, lawyer) makes binding decision on facts

5. Arbitration

Formal arbitration process with binding decision by arbitrator

6. Buyout Option

Dissatisfied shareholder can require company or other shareholders to buy their shares

Key Principle: Start with family-friendly methods and escalate to formal procedures only when necessary. The goal is to preserve both business value and family relationships.

üö™ Exit Strategies for Family Shareholders

Planning for when shareholders want or need to leave the business

Right of First Refusal

Other shareholders get first chance to buy shares before they're sold to outsiders

  • Protects family control
  • Provides fair pricing mechanism
  • Requires prompt response timelines
  • Common in family business agreements

Shotgun Clause

Shareholder offers to sell at a price, buyer can accept or buy at same price

  • Forces fair valuation
  • Resolves deadlock situations
  • Requires independent valuation
  • Can be emotionally charged

Drag-Along Rights

Majority shareholders can force minority to sell if they receive good offer

  • Enables third-party sales
  • Protects majority interests
  • Requires supermajority approval
  • Provides liquidity option

Tag-Along Rights

Minority shareholders can join majority sale on same terms

  • Protects minority interests
  • Ensures fair treatment
  • Prevents majority from getting better deals
  • Requires same price and terms

Compulsory Buyout

Company or other shareholders must buy shares in specific circumstances

  • For breach of agreement
  • Death or disability
  • Divorce or family disputes
  • Failure to meet obligations

Put Option

Shareholder can require company to buy their shares at agreed price

  • Provides liquidity guarantee
  • Requires funding availability
  • Triggers at specific events
  • Prevents forced retention

üí∞ Share Valuation Methods

How to determine fair value when shares need to be bought or sold

Independent Valuation

Professional valuer determines fair market value

  • Most accurate method
  • Required for major transactions
  • Can be expensive
  • Provides dispute resolution

Formula-Based

Pre-agreed formula using financial metrics

  • Multiple of earnings
  • Asset-based valuation
  • Discounted cash flow
  • Predictable and fair

Book Value

Based on balance sheet net asset value

  • Simple to calculate
  • May not reflect true value
  • Good for stable businesses
  • Tax-efficient for transfers

Fixed Price

Pre-determined price per share

  • Simple and predictable
  • May become outdated
  • Requires regular updates
  • Good for estate planning

Best Practice: Use independent valuation for significant transactions and formula-based methods for routine transfers. Always include dispute resolution procedures for valuation disagreements.
